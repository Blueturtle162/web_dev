Lupus

Description

Lupus is an autoimmune condition that causes the immune system to attack itself. It can cause damage to major organs, tissue, skin, joints and blood cells. There is currently no cure for lupus but there are various treatments. Lupus can be caused by a variety of factors but most people contract lupus because of their genetics or environmental factors like smoking and certain medications.

Signs and Symptoms

There are multiple signs and symptoms of lupus. Common signs include: fatigue, joint pain, fever, a butterfly shaped rash, sunlight sensitivity, hair and weight loss, and mouth sores. Some rarer symptoms can include: chest pain, kidney complications, heart complications, blood clots, seizures, mental difficulties, and fingers and toes tuning white or blue. Other complications from lupus can include: muscle pain, swollen lymph nodes, anemia, low platelet count, and intestinal problems. Symptoms can vary and depend on the amount of time the patient has had lupus.

Who is at risk

You are more at risk of contracting Lupus if you:

  • Are female
  • Have a family history of Lupus
  • Are African American
  • Are Hispanic
  • Are Asian
